Description

Spaghetti bolognese is a world-famous Italian pasta dish featuring long, thin pasta strands topped with a rich, slow-cooked meat sauce. The authentic sauce (ragù) originates from Bologna and combines minced meat, a aromatic base of finely diced vegetables, tomatoes, and wine into a thick, savory sauce. It stands as one of the most popular and beloved comfort foods globally.

Ingredients

For the pasta

  • Spaghetti: 500gr

For the sauce

  • minced meat: 500gr (beef or beef/pork mix)
  • pancetta or bacon: 100gr (cubed)
  • 1 large onion: finely diced
  • 1 large carrot: finely diced
  • 1 celery stalk: finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic: minced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • canned diced tomatoes: 800gr
  • dry red or white wine: 150ml
  • beef stock: 150ml, (or whole milk for an authentic smooth texture)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • salt and black pepper

For serving

  • parmesan cheese
  • a few leaves fresh basil

Cooking instructions

Prepare the vegetables

  1. Chop the onion, carrot, and celery into very small, even cubes.
  2. Mince the garlic cloves.

Sear the meat

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large, deep pan or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Fry the cubed pancetta or bacon for 3-4 minutes until crisp.
  3. Add the minced meat, breaking it up with a wooden spoon.
  4. Cook until the meat is completely browned, then remove everything from the pan and set aside, leaving the fat in the pan.

Cook the Base (Sofrito)

  1. Turn the heat down to medium-low.
  2. Add the diced onion, carrot, and celery to the remaining fat.
  3. Sauté for about 8-10 minutes until the vegetables are soft and translucent.
  4. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for 1 more minute.

Build the sauce

  1. Return the meat and bacon to the pan with the vegetables.
  2. Add the tomato paste and stir well for 2 minutes to caramelize it.
  3. Pour in the wine to deglaze the pan, scraping up all the flavorful bits from the bottom.
  4. Simmer until the wine has reduced by half.

Simmer and mature

  1. Pour in the canned tomatoes and beef stock (or milk).
  2. Add the bay leaves and dried oregano.
  3. Season with a pinch of salt and black pepper.
  4. Cover with a lid, turn the heat to low, and simmer for at least 1 to 2 hours. Stir occasionally.

Cook the pasta and serve

  1. Boil a large pot of salted water 15 minutes before the sauce is done.
  2. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente.
  3. Drain the pasta, reserving a splash of pasta water to loosen the sauce if needed.
  4. Mix the spaghetti directly into the sauce, or ladle the sauce over individual portions.
  5. Garnish with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves.
